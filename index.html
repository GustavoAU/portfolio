How is the project workflow organised?

  1. Research and analysis

    The first stage involves an in-depth analysis of the target audience and competitors. An important part of this stage is UX research, which helps to understand user needs and identify key features that will need to be considered in the design

  2. UX design

    At this stage, the structure of the site/application is determiend, teh page map and their interaction scenarios are worked out. Content is also worked out iin detail and prototypes of future pages are created.

  3. UI design

    After the general concept is approved, the visual style of the product is developed: logo, color palette, fonts and graphic elements. Futher, on its basis, page/screen designs are created. They are tested for usability and, if necessary, finalized.

  4. Development & Testing

    Includes page/screen layout, technical implementation of functionality and SEO-optimization. At this stage, the domain, analytics systems and integration with other services are connected. The final product is tested on various scenarios to exclude the occurrence of errors

  5. Efficiency assessment

    After the launch of the project, key metrics are collected and analyzed. This allows you to evaluate the effectiveness of the design to make adjustments to improve the product.

What is your role in the project?

I am engaged in research, UX/IU design and web development.
Also, the project may require other work - to write text, draw unique illustrations or develop functionality on a certain stack. Depending on the tasks, I can involve a copywriter, graphic designer, developers and other specialists.

If the project already has a team, I am ready to join it as a product designer.

What is the advantage of the tools you use?

Figma - for creating designs

I use Figma for interface design and prototyping. It is a collaborative design tool that allows me to create and animate design layouts on a professional level. I can show you the progress of work in real time and coordinate the visuals with you.

Tilda and Webflow - for website edevelopment

Tilda is a versatile builder that can be used to quickly test hypotheses or launch a landing page or online store. It is ideal for small businesses that need a website without strong functionality. You can edit the content of Tilda sites yourself, without the need for a programmer.

Webflow is a more professional website development tool that can be used to create beautiful and functional websites with high performance and CMS support. It is suitable for larger companies with higher requirements for development capabilities and performance. I will help you determine the platform to be used according to your tasks and requirements at the start of the project.

When should you consider an UX-audit?

  • The site has good traffic, but low conversion rate

    Metrics say that a significant number of people visit site, but only a small percentage take a target action

  • Poor user experience

    The site navigation is confusion for both you and your visitors

  • High cost per lead

    You invest in advertising, but user click on your ads without taking the targeted action

  • High bounce rate

    A high percentage of user leave the site after only a few seconds

  • No understanding of how to boost the site

    You are not sure what kind of improvements will benefits users
